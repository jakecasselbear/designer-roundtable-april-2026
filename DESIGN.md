# Design Brief
**Collab: Designer Roundtable — April 2026**

---

## North Star

The project centers on three guiding concepts that should inform every design decision:

- **Hometown Heritage** — rooted in local identity and community pride
- **Authentic Italian** — genuine, old-world Italian character, not a caricature
- **Hometown High-End** — elevated quality without losing neighborhood warmth

---

## Feeling & Mood

The emotional target is intimate and personal — like sitting down for wine with Nonna. The experience should feel:

- Welcoming, inviting, friendly, and full of heritage
- Like a high-end caliber experience nestled inside a small deli
- **Home Made** and **Fresh** — never polished to the point of feeling manufactured

---

## Color Palette

| Color | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Tomato Red** | Primary brand color |
| **Very Dark Warm Gray** | Use in place of pure black for warmth |
| **Leo's Latticini Green** | `#18a293` — a teal-green accent drawn from reference imagery |

---

## Typography

**Primary candidates:**
- **Newsreader** — editorial, heritage feel
- **ITC Busorama / ITC Benguiat** — expressive, period-appropriate display faces

**Supporting/accent:**
- **Italiano** — for Italian-language details or decorative use
- **Cooper** — friendly, rounded, warm

---

## UI & Components

- **Hamburger menu icon** → replaced with a deli sandwich illustration
- **Rounded buttons** throughout — no sharp corners
- **Loading animation** → a spinning Parmesan wheel
- **Cursor** → changes to a 🥪 sandwich emoji on interactive links

---

## Visual Elements

- Woodblock illustration of a rolling Italian countryside
- Red checker tablecloth textures
- Memorabilia (photos, artifacts, vintage objects)
- Parmesan loading wheel (doubles as a UI element and a visual motif)

---

## Features

- **Isometric "Build Your Own Sandwich"** — an interactive feature where users select ingredients and watch them stack onto a sandwich in real time (isometric view)
- The site has lots of chefs — lean into the team/craft story

---

## Design Dos & Don'ts

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|---|---|
| Feel warm, personal, and neighborhood-rooted | Feel corporate or sterile |
| Use rich, layered layouts with texture | Over-use white space / negative space |
| Embrace heritage and handmade aesthetics | Look like a generic restaurant template |
